EXPLORING CAT DRAWING ABILITIES: LLMs IN P5.JS

Introduction and Methods:

Embark on a captivating exploration of cat drawing skills as we delve into the creative realm of Language Models (LLMs) in conjunction with p5.js. In this intriguing test, we invite you to witness the artistic prowess of three remarkable LLMs: ChatGPT , BingAI and Bard.

By presenting each LLM with the prompt "draw a cat for me in p5.js code" and examining the resulting artwork, we unravel their distinct artistic capabilities, styles, and interpretations. Join us on this visual voyage as we compare and analyze the captivating cat drawings brought to life by ChatGPT, BingAI, and Bard within the dynamic landscape of p5.js . Results:

In the drawing generated by ChatGPT, we observe a white cat depicted from a frontal perspective. The head of the cat is well-proportioned, with distinct eyes, nose, and mouth. However, the ears appear as large floating triangles, slightly disconnected from the head and off-center. Below the head, the trunk of the cat is properly proportioned, and the feet are correctly positioned. However, the attempt to draw a tail results in a triangle shape that is far to the right of the body, disconnected from any anatomical structure.

In the drawing generated by Bing AI, we see an orange cat illustrated from a side view. The most noticeable feature is the disproportionately small head in relation to the body. The ears are small triangles correctly positioned on the head. The cat has small eyes, a nose, and precisely three whiskers. However, the arrangement of the whiskers and their limited number creates a somewhat unsettling facial expression. The trunk of the cat is significantly larger than the head, and there are no indications of legs or a tail.

The drawing generated by Bard does not resemble a cat at all. It consists of two small red circles positioned above a black circle, which, in turn, sits on top of an orange circle. There are no recognizable features such as eyes, mouth, ears, legs, tail, or whiskers. The overall representation does not convey any semblance of a cat, making it challenging for a human viewer to interpret it as such.

Conclusion:

In the comparison of the cat drawings generated by different LLMs, we observe varying degrees of success in capturing the essence of a cat. ChatGPT's drawing demonstrates relatively accurate proportions of the head and body, although the disconnected and misaligned ears and tail detract from its overall realism. Bing AI's drawing exhibits a notable discrepancy in head-to-body ratio, accompanied by an unsettling facial expression due to the limited and misplaced whiskers. Bard's drawing fails to capture any recognizable features of a cat, resulting in an abstract representation that bears no resemblance to the intended subject.

While these LLMs have the potential to generate cat drawings, their outputs display limitations in terms of anatomical accuracy and coherent representation. Further refinements and training could potentially improve their capabilities. However, it is evident that there are significant challenges in capturing the nuanced details and complexity of cat drawings through AI-generated imagery.
